Research Experience

Current Research – I am currently a part of the GeoAIR Lab (Geospatial Artificial Intelligence Research and Visualization Laboratory) and Dr.Liping Yang. Learn more about the lab here

2022 - In Dr. Liping Yang's Internet Mapping course I led a team that developed an interactive web map showing riparian vegetation along the Northern Rio Grande corridor. The application can be found here. A video introduction and explanation of the application can be found here.

2021 - I performed a spatial analysis project evaluating the hydrology of a mesa top in Central New Mexico. The mesa contains the archaeological remnants of dry farming fields cultivated by Ancestral Puebloan people. I visually located the fields on aerial footage and created a map. Using ArcGIS's Spatial Analysis tools I created watersheds out of the ephemeral arroyos and overlaid these above field polygons. This modeled how water would have run over the fields before running off the mesa. To simulate the amount of sunlight the fields received I created three Hillshade rasters that represented the sun in the morning, noon and afternoon. The model did not use paleoenvironmental data. To take the research further a map of the arroyos would need to be made at the time of occupation. Archaeological presence of check dams or other agricultural techniques would need to be taken into consideration.

2021 – Through the UNM Intro to GIS course under Dr. Caitlin Lippitt, I took part in an urban tree study. My part in this study focused on a three-block study area in the residential part of Nob Hill, Albuquerque. I took photos of the non-cultivated vegetation within the study area and monitored it over the semester. Ultimately, all of the trees in the study area were digitally mapped, this added to the class’s overall understanding of Albuquerque’s urban canopy.

2017 – The UNT Anthropology Field Methods class created and took part in a research project that gained and presented community input to the city of Denton, Texas in anticipation for the cities new plan and budget. This research was advised by Dr. Andrew Nelson and done in partnership with the city of Denton. In participation with this research, I observed a study area, surveyed stakeholders within that area, interviewed multiple residents of the area, analyzed data, wrote a report of findings and presented those findings.

2014 – As part of an undergraduate course focused on applied and activist anthropology I took part in a research project on human trafficking awareness in Denton, Texas. The research was advised by Dr. Mariela Nuñez-Janes and worked in partnership with a local non-profit ODIS (Opening Doors International Services). This research included working with multiple stakeholders, creating a research plan, conducting surveys, conducting interviews, data analysis, report presentation and the writing of a report.
