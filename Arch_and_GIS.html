Interests in Archaeology:


I have broad interests in Archaeology. Generally, in Anthropology I am interested in egalitarian lifeways, third genders globally, community engagement and community informed action. As anthropology is a discipline that was born out of and sought to justify colonialism, I find it important to fight against the neo-colonial influences on the discipline currently. As a white archaeologist, I plan to follow the lead of archaeologists of color, and activists from involved communities. In relation to this I am interested in Historical Archaeology, specifically shedding light on the lives of Indigenous, Black and other marginalized peoples in American history. Theoretically, I am interested in Marxist Archaeology, and I seek to think of all kinds of people from the past. This can be done by studying the archaeology of households and labor. I want to use the web development skills I have gained to create accessible, scholarly archaeological knowledge for the public.
I am very excited to work in the field, as not only do I love archaeology, but I love being outside. I found myself on hikes, sitting on the ground and running my hands through the dirt. So why not be paid to shovel! In addition to the field, I look forward to working in the lab. Be it artifact analysis, geochemical testing, or research for reports.

Research Experience

Current Research – I am currently working on a research project about Ancestral Puebloan agricultural features in New Mexico. This project is using data from the UNM 1990 Field School and the UNM Office of Contract Archaeology. I will be creating a geospatial database, mapping features, and performing spatial analysis to understand the hydrology of these features. I am working with Dr. W.H. Wills.

2021 – Through the UNM Intro to GIS course under Dr. Caitlin Lippitt, I took part in an urban tree study. My part in this study focused on a three-block study area in the residential part of Nob Hill, Albuquerque. I took photos of the non-cultivated vegetation within the study area and monitored it over the semester. Ultimately, all of the trees in the study area were digitally mapped, this added to the class’s overall understanding of Albuquerque’s urban canopy.

2017 – The UNT Anthropology Field Methods class created and took part in a research project that gained and presented community input to the city of Denton, Texas in anticipation for the cities new plan and budget. This research was advised by Dr. Andrew Nelson and done in partnership with the city of Denton. In participation with this research, I observed a study area, surveyed stakeholders within that area, interviewed multiple residents of the area, analyzed data, wrote a report of findings and presented those findings.

2014 – As part of an undergraduate course focused on applied and activist anthropology I took part in a research project on human trafficking awareness in Denton, Texas. The research was advised by Dr. Mariela Nuñez-Janes and worked in partnership with a local non-profit ODIS (Opening Doors International Services). This research included working with multiple stakeholders, creating a research plan, conducting surveys, conducting interviews, data analysis, report presentation and the writing of a report.
